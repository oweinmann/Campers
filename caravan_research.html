üìã Project Overview

Your Requirements

  • Length: 15-18 feet (4.57-5.49 meters)
  • Condition: New only (not used)
  • Budget: Maximum $100,000 AUD
  • Sleeping: Minimum 2 bunk beds for children
  • Roof: Hard top only (no hybrid/pop-tops)
  • Capability: Off-road capable
üìä Research Summary: This guide covers 35+ manufacturers (Australian-made and imported), with 24 priority models identified that likely meet your requirements. Potential savings of $20,000-$40,000 available with imported brands.

  • Bed dimensions (main & bunks)

üÜö Australian-Made vs Imported: Complete Comparison

The Big Question: Should you pay $20,000-$40,000 more for Australian-made quality and support, or save significantly with an imported brand? This section helps you decide.

üíµ Price Comparison

Category Australian-Made Imported Savings
Budget Models $65,000 - $85,000 $45,000 - $65,000 $20,000
Mid-Range Models $75,000 - $95,000 $55,000 - $80,000 $20-30,000
Premium Models $90,000 - $120,000 $70,000 - $90,000 $30-40,000

‚úÖ Australian-Made Advantages

  • Build Quality: Higher quality control, better fit and finish
  • Support Network: Extensive dealer and service centers nationwide
  • Resale Value: Hold value better (typically 50% after 5 years)
  • Warranty: 2-5 year warranties, easier claims process
  • Parts: Better availability across Australia
  • Longevity: Established brands with proven track records
  • Australian Conditions: Designed for local climate and roads

‚úÖ Imported Advantages

  • Price: $20,000-$40,000 cheaper upfront
  • Features: More standard inclusions for the price
  • Modern Design: Contemporary styling and layouts
  • Lower Investment: Less financial commitment
  • Accessory Budget: More money for upgrades (solar, awning, etc.)
  • Dollar Depreciation: Lose less in absolute dollars
  • First-Timer Friendly: Lower risk while testing lifestyle

ü§î Decision Matrix: Which Should You Choose?

Choose Australian-Made If:

  • ‚úì Planning long-term ownership (5-10+ years)
  • ‚úì Frequent travel to remote areas
  • ‚úì Value warranty and support network
  • ‚úì Plan to resell in future
  • ‚úì Want established quality/reliability
  • ‚úì Budget allows ($75k-100k)
  • ‚úì Peace of mind over savings

Best Picks: New Age Manta Ray, Crusader Musketeer, Lotus Trooper

Choose Imported If:

  • ‚úì Budget conscious (save $20k-40k)
  • ‚úì First caravan purchase
  • ‚úì Careful buyer (will inspect thoroughly)
  • ‚úì Handy with minor repairs
  • ‚úì Want more features for less
  • ‚úì Plan shorter ownership (3-5 years)
  • ‚úì Use savings for upgrades

Best Picks: Retreat Hamilton/Fraser, Evernew Family, On The Move

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Questions for Imported Brands

  1. Is warranty backed by Australian company or overseas manufacturer?
  2. What exactly is covered and what is excluded?
  3. Who performs warranty work and where?
  4. How many service centers in Australia?
  5. Has company been operating in Australia for 3+ years?
  6. Can you speak with existing owners?
  7. What is parts availability like?
  8. Are appliances from known brands (Dometic, Thetford, etc.)?

üî§ Australian Caravan Terminology

Term Definition Why It Matters
Tare Weight Empty weight of caravan (no water, gas, supplies) Base weight before you add anything
ATM Aggregate Trailer Mass - Maximum total weight fully loaded Must not exceed your vehicle's towing capacity
Ball Weight Downward force on tow ball (typically 10-15% of ATM) Must be within your vehicle's ball weight limit
Payload ATM minus Tare - How much you can load Determines how much gear/water/food you can carry
DO35/DO45 Popular Australian off-road coupling systems Better for off-road than standard ball coupling
Checker Plate Diamond-pattern protective metal sheeting Protects chassis and storage areas
MPPT Maximum Power Point Tracking solar controller More efficient than PWM controllers
AGM Battery Absorbed Glass Mat battery Cheaper, heavier, shorter life than Lithium
LiFePO4 Lithium Iron Phosphate battery Lighter, longer life, more expensive than AGM
