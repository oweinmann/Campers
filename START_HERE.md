3. **Goldstream Storm/Tornado** ($70k-95k) - Off-road focused

5. **Jurgens Lunagazer** ($70k-90k) - Lightweight, established brand

### Best Value Imported (Meeting Requirements):
1. **Retreat Caravans - Hamilton/Fraser** ($55k-80k) - Good reputation
2. **Concept Caravans - Ascot/Euro** ($60k-85k) - Modern designs
3. **On The Move - BLADE/TRAXX** ($50k-75k) - Growing brand, off-road focus
4. **Evernew - Family series** ($45k-70k) - Budget-friendly, well under $100k

### The Big Decision: Australian vs Imported?

**Choose Australian if:**
- Long-term ownership (5-10+ years)
- Frequent remote travel
- Value warranty and service network
- Plan to resell
- Can afford $70k-100k

**Choose Imported if:**
- Budget conscious ($45k-80k)
- First caravan (testing lifestyle)
- Want more features for less money
- Willing to inspect thoroughly
- Can handle potential minor issues

**Potential Savings with Imported:** $20,000-40,000

## Important Notes

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Models to Avoid:
- **Too Long**: Phoenix RV (21ft), JB Scorpion (21ft), Option RV Traction (21ft)
- **Hybrid Roof**: Track Trailer T4 Hybrid, any pop-top models
- **Over Budget**: Kedron ($90k+), Bushtracker ($100k+), Kimberley ($100k+)

### üí° Key Research Tips:
1. Always ask if the layout includes bunks - not all models do
2. Confirm hard top (many off-road models are hybrid)
3. Get the ATM weight - ensure your tow vehicle can handle it
4. Solar/battery upgrades often worthwhile
5. Attend caravan shows for promotions and side-by-side comparison
6. Join Facebook groups for specific brands before buying

### üìç Caravan Shows (Best Time to Buy):

- Melbourne Leisurefest
- Brisbane Caravan, Camping & Touring Supershow

**Show benefits:** Promotional pricing, compare multiple brands, special deals
